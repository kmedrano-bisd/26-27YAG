Track Divergence & Strategic Notes

Side-by-side comparison of where the 6th Honors curriculum diverges to prepare students for accelerated pathways.

Topic Area Target Dates 6th On-Level Pace 6th Honors Pace Teacher Differentiation Strategy
Factors & Multiples August Focuses on numbers: GCF, LCM, and simple prime values. Moves fast into prime bases with multi-step exponents. Honors will jump to abstract algebraic layouts much sooner than On-Level.
Ratios & Proportions October Uses model layouts: Tape diagrams and double number lines. Moves directly into 7th Grade constant rates ($y=kx$). On-Level requires visual models; Honors needs function equations and slope connections.
The Coordinate Plane December Focuses mostly on Quadrant 1, moving into matching reflections. Full 4-quadrant manipulation, including geometric coordinate distance. Ensure Honors connects graphing solutions smoothly back to inequality constraints.
Area & Geometry Structures Jan & Bridge Decomposes base polygons (triangles, rectangles, trapezoids). Pulls in 7th grade circle area ($A = \pi r^2$) and circumference tracking. On-Level works with straight lines; Honors handles curves, pi, and radius metrics.
Linear Relationships & Functions February Identifies input/output rows and additive vs multiplicative scales. Calculates non-proportional graphs ($y=mx+b$) and solves equations. Honors completely skips simple practice loops to establish multi-step linear algebra.
Bridge to 7th Honors Late April / May Since ALL 6th enters 7th Honors, pacing unifies into 7th standards. Full 7th grade TEKS alignment for geometry and proportional limits. Unify the pacing here. Everyone needs to master circle equations and advanced geometry.
